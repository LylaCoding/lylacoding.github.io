Blood Testing

When taking hormones it is important that you are doing regular blood tests. (Usually every 3-6 months, depending on how long you have been taking hormones for.)

This guide will go over, what blood tests you should be getting, what target hormone ranges you should look for in your blood tests results, where you can get blood tests done, and how taking certain hormones will affect the levels of your other hormones

Where can I get blood tests done?

Knowing where to get your blood tests done can be tricky as not everyone will have support of their doctors when self-medicating. However, there are multiple options available for getting blood tests.

Your Local GP
If your local GP is supportive of self-medication, they may be able to provide you with blood tests. This is the most convenient option as it is free and easier to get your results back.
There is a crowdsourced list of GPs you can find here that are Trans+ friendly, where you can search for a GP near you that has a history of performing hormone blood tests with comments from people's experiences there.
Note: while your doctor may be willing to give you a blood test they may not always be willing to interpret the results for you.

CliniQ London
CliniQ is a clinic in London that provides free blood tests for trans and non-binary people every Tuesday from 4:00pm to 6:30pm. They are a great option if you are unable to get blood tests from your GP. You can access their website here.
Note: CliniQ is an appointment only clinic, so you will need to book an appointment before going.

56 Dean Street
56 Dean Street is a clinic in London that provides free blood tests for trans and non-binary people every Wednesday from 4:30pm-7:00pm. You can access their website here.
Note: 56 Dean Street is an appointment only clinic, so you will need to book an appointment before going.

What blood tests should I be getting?

Baseline blood tests For people taking either Estrogen or Testosterone are:
  • Full blood count
  • Liver Function Test
  • HbA1c
  • Fasting lipids
  • Prolactin
  • Luteinising hormone
  • Follicle stimulating hormone
  • Testosterone
  • Oestradiol
  • Sex hormone binding globulin
  • Vitamin D
Repeat Blood tests are as follows:

For those taking Estrogen:
  • Liver function test
  • HbA1c
  • Fasting lipids
  • Prolactin
  • Estradiol
  • Testosterone
For those taking Testosterone:
  • Liver function test
  • HbA1c
  • Fasting lipids
  • Testosterone
  • Full Blood Count
